Doris Lessing

"Whatever you're meant to do, do it now. The conditions are always impossible."

Here's a time line of Doris Lessing's life:

  • 1919 Iran, Kermanshah - Born in Iran to British parents

  • 1925 South Rhodesia, Salisbury - She and her family moves to the British colony of Southern Rhodesia. There she was educated at the Dominican Convent High School in Salisbury the capital of Southern Rhodesian. She left school at age 13 and was self-educated from then on.

  • 1934 South Rhodesia, Salisbury - She left home and worked as a nursemaid. During these years here employer gave her material on polititcs and sociology to read and she began writing and selling her stories to magazines.

  • 1937 South Rhodesia, Salisbury - Moved to Salisbury to work as a telephone operator where she met her first husband (civil servant Frank Wisdom). She had two children (John and Jean) with him before the marriage ended in 1943. She left the family home in 1943, leaving her children with their father.

  • 1943-49 South Rhodesia, Salisbury - She had two children with her husband before the marriage ended in 1943. She left the family home in 1943, leaving her children with their father. In the same year she married Gottfried Lessing whom she met in the Left Book CLub in Salisbury. They had one child (Peter) together. In the same period she also had a love affair with John Whitehorn. Since 1944 she was a communist.

  • 1949 UK, London - She moved with her son Peter to London to pursue her writing career and socialist beliefs, there she became a member of the British Communist Party.

  • 1950 UK, London - Her first novel 'The Grass is Singing' which is set in Southern Rhodesia(today Zimbabwe)was published. During her Communist phase she wrote radically about social issues.

  • 1956 UK, London - She leaves the British Communist Party following the Soviet invasion of Hungary and this marks the beginning of her psychological phase which leads to several novels.

  • 1962 UK, London - With the novel 'The Golden Notebook' she gained international attention. And many literary scientists regard it as her major work.
  • 1965 UK, London - Idries Shah founded the Society for Understanding Fundamental Ideas (SUFI) an educational charity. Doris Lessing counts him as a good friend and he introduced her to the Sufism which is nowadays described as a wide range of social, cultural, political and religious phenomena associated with the Sufis. This marks also the end of her pyschological phase in 1969 and is then followed by the Sufi phase.
  • 1979-1983 UK, London - She publishes the Canopus sequence. It presents an advanced interstellar society's efforts to accelerate the evolution of other worlds, including Earth. She always counted it as the most meaningful work in her life.
  • 1983-2007 UK, London - Henceforth she keeps herself busy with the 3 topics which impressed her the most: 'Socialism', 'Psychology' and 'Sufism' which finds way in many of her works.
  • 2007 UK, London - Doris Lessing was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature. At the time of the award she was at the age of 88 years the oldest winner of the literature prize. She was the eleventh woman to be awarded with it.
  • 2013 UK, London - She died at her home in London, predeceased by her two sons but was survived by her daughter. She was remembered with a humanist funeral service.
